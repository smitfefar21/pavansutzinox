Why Zinc Oxide is Essential in Chemical Manufacturing

Zinc Oxide serves as a versatile functional material in numerous chemical processes due to its unique physicochemical properties. Our high purity Zinc Oxide (up to 99.60%) is specially processed for chemical applications.

Key Functions in Chemical Processes:

  • Catalyst: Accelerates reactions in organic synthesis and petrochemical processes
  • Precursor: Raw material for zinc compounds production
  • Vulcanization Activator: Essential for rubber and polymer chemistry
  • Gas Purification: Removes sulfur compounds from industrial gases
  • Electronics: Key material in varistors and semiconductors
  • Ceramics: Modifies thermal and electrical properties

Catalytic Applications

Zinc Oxide as catalyst in key industrial processes:

  • Methanol Synthesis: ZnO-Cr2O3 catalysts for CO/CO2 hydrogenation
  • Desulfurization: Hydrodesulfurization catalyst component
  • Oxidation Reactions: Selective oxidation of hydrocarbons
  • Polymerization: Catalyst for polyester and polyurethane production
  • Photocatalysis: UV-activated degradation of organic pollutants

Typical specifications: Surface area 10-50 m2/g, pore volume 0.2-0.5 cm3/g

Chemical Precursor

Raw material for zinc compound production:

  • Zinc Salts: Production of zinc sulfate, chloride, and acetate
  • Zinc Stearate: Lubricant and release agent manufacturing
  • Zinc Phosphates: Corrosion-resistant coating formulations
  • Zinc Peroxide: Oxidizing agent production
  • Other Compounds: Zinc borate, zinc silicate, etc.

Key requirement: High chemical reactivity and purity

Gas Treatment

Gas purification and desulfurization applications:

  • H2S Removal: Reacts with hydrogen sulfide in gas streams
  • SO2 Absorption: Flue gas desulfurization processes
  • Mercury Capture: Removes Hg from industrial emissions
  • Gas Sweetening: Purification of natural gas and syngas

Optimal characteristics: High porosity and surface reactivity

Electronic Applications

Specialty applications in electronics industry:

  • Varistors: ZnO-based surge protection devices
  • Transparent Conductors: Doped ZnO for optoelectronics
  • Piezoelectrics: ZnO thin films for sensors
  • Semiconductors: II-VI semiconductor applications
  • Phosphors: Component in luminescent materials

Critical parameters: Controlled dopants, particle size distribution
